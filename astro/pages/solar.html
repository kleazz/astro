The Sun makes a full rotation once every 25-35 days. Its equator rotates faster than its poles, and this is known as differential rotation.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. It is home to millions of species, including humans, and features a diverse range of landscapes from oceans to mountains.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and is known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth that has been raging for centuries. It has more than 75 moons and a faint ring system.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance caused by iron oxide on its surface. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our Solar System and is closest to the Sun. It has a cratered surface and extreme temperature variations between its day and night sides.

Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System, and it plays a crucial role in the stability of Earth's axial tilt and the tidal patterns on our planet.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our Solar System. It is a gas giant and has the strongest sustained winds of any planet in the Solar System, reaching speeds of up to 1,300 mph.

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, far beyond the orbit of Neptune. It was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006, and it has a complex, highly elliptical orbit that takes it closer to the Sun than Neptune for about 20 years out of its 248-year orbit.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our Solar System, after Jupiter. It is known for its extensive ring system, made up mostly of ice particles and dust. Saturn has 82 moons, the largest of which is Titan.

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It is almost perfectly spherical and consists of hot plasma interwoven with magnetic fields. The Sun's energy supports almost all life on Earth through photosynthesis and drives our planet's climate and weather.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is unique among the planets because it rotates on its side. It has a faint ring system and 27 known moons. Uranus is an ice giant, similar in composition to Neptune, with hydrogen and helium making up most of its atmosphere.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size, mass, and proximity to the Sun. It has a thick atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our Solar System.
